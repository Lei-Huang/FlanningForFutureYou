What employers look for?

COMMUNICATION

Being a good listener, expressing clearly, concisely and persuasively and communicating well with different audiences.

TEAMWORK

Working well with others to achieve results and recognising the value of other people's contributions and ideas.

PROBLEM-SOLVING

Being able to think critically and logically and identify solutions to arrive at a favourable outcome.

INITIATIVE

Being able to see innovative ways of doing things, seizing opportunities, adapting to new situations and taking initiative.

PLANNING and ORGANISING

Managing time efficiently, establishing clear goals and objectives and prioritising what tasks need to be done to achieve a goal.

SELF-MANAGEMENT

Having a personal vision and goals, being able to evaluate and monitor own performance, having the confidence and ability to take responsibility for actions.

You should know:

Skills

Develop your transferrable skills.

Academia: Public speaking, Research, Publications, Fellowshipsg

Not for Profit/ Public Sector/ Private Sector: Communication, Teamwork, Analytical and problem solving, Negotiation

Networking

Get involved, join clubs and societies or volunteer.

Connect with your lecturers, tutors and fellow students.

Academia: NECTAR, Attend and get involved at conferences, Close connections

Not for Profit: Volunteer or join committees / boards / counsel / areas of interest / associations, Attend seminars, Work experience

Public Sector: Attend / get involved in conferences, Internships / vacation programs, Attend APS / career fairs (e.g. Big Meet and Tertiary to Work), Attend seminars run by APSC

Private Sector: Attend / get involved in conferences, Internships / vacation programs, Attend career fairs (e.g. Big Meet and Tertiary to Work)

Experience

Get involved in an association/community.

Look for volunteer or casual opportunities.

Academia: Present at and attend conferences, 3 Minute Thesis, CHELT teaching certificates, Guest lecturing, Tutoring / ACSA

Not for Profit/ Public Sector/ Private Sector: University degree, Work in an office to gain transferrable skills, Leadership roles on student associations, Get involved (e.g. Woroni, ANUSA or PARSA)

Preparation

Look for cadetships and employment opportunities.

Research career paths, jobs and employers, attend career events.

Find casual work.

Academia/ Not for Profit/ Public Sector/ Private Sector: Archival and specialized CV, Career planning throughout degree, Research organisation, Network
